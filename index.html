About

My name is Mahyar Maleki, and I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Tehran. While taking the cognitive science course at the university, I discovered a deep interest in neuroscience, particularly in the field of visual perception. This realization led me to define my master's thesis in this area. Currently, I am actively engaged in my master's thesis, which focuses on analyzing EEG data induced by video stimuli and comparing it with still image stimuli. The objective of my research is to investigate how the representation of visual data in the brain changes when transitioning from still images to videos and incorporating temporal and spatial features instead of solely spatial features. By exploring the effects of video stimuli on EEG data, I hope to shed light on the dynamic nature of visual perception and its implications for artificial intelligence and cognitive science.
